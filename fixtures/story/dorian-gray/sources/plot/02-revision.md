# Plot Outline — v2 (revision)

Working title: *The Picture of Dorian Gray*
Scope: Chapters I–III.

This is a revision of `01-outline.md`. The shape is broadly the same.
The differences are in emphasis. Where v1 framed Basil's refusal to
exhibit as a craftsman's reluctance, here it is reframed as a fear of
self-revelation: the painter is afraid of being seen, not of being
imitated. Two beats are altered, and one symbolic detail is added.

---

## Act 1 — The Studio in Summer

- Hallward and Lord Henry, in the studio, before the finished portrait.
- The garden is fully in flower. The scent of roses dominates. Lilac
  and laburnum are noted but secondary.
- **New symbolic detail:** the *pink-flowering thorn* by the studio
  door. It is the first thing one smells coming in. It is to be read
  as a small foreshadowing — beauty with a barb in it.
- Lord Henry presses Basil to exhibit. Basil refuses.
- *(Cut from v1: the silver cigarette case. The detail was clever but
  did not earn its keep. The painter's hands will simply be still.)*

## Act 2 — The Painter's Confession (reframed)

- The refusal to exhibit is no longer the refusal of an artist who
  resents being copied. It is the refusal of a man who has, in the
  picture, *confessed* something. He has shown in it "the secret of
  his own soul." The line is to be kept verbatim if it can be made to
  sound natural.
- The painter's fear is not professional. It is moral and personal.
  This is the load-bearing change in the revision.
- Hallward asks Lord Henry not to spoil the boy. Lord Henry promises
  nothing.

## Act 3 — Dorian Arrives

- Dorian is found at the piano in the studio. He has come early.
- Basil tries to send Lord Henry away; Dorian asks him to stay; Basil
  yields.
- The sitting begins. Lord Henry talks. Dorian listens.
- *(Moved from Act 4 in v1:* the line about Time being jealous of
  beauty is to be planted here, in the studio, not in the garden.
  This shifts the centre of gravity of Lord Henry's seduction earlier.*)*

## Act 4 — The Garden and the Wish

- Lord Henry takes Dorian into the garden. The garden scene is now
  shorter. Its function is to let the heat and the scent of the
  flowers work on the boy, not to deliver the seduction; that has
  already been delivered in the studio.
- The portrait is uncovered. Dorian sees himself.
- The wish: he would give his soul if the picture could bear his age
  for him. This stays. It is the book's hinge.
- Basil's reaction is a shade more violent in this draft. He nearly
  destroys the canvas with a palette knife and is stopped only by
  Dorian himself. *(Whether this stays will depend on how it reads.)*

## Act 5 — The Uncle in the Albany

- Lord Henry walks from Curzon Street to the Albany.
- Lord Fermor in his rooms: a study of comfortable English misanthropy.
- The lineage is delivered: Margaret Devereux; the runaway marriage; a
  duel; a death; a boy left to a hard old grandfather.
- Henry leaves to walk to lunch in **Berkeley Square**, turning the
  story over in his mind. The walk is the first sign that Dorian has
  become, for him, a project.

---

## Notes for the prose

- The pink thorn (Act 1, new) is to recur. If it cannot be made to
  recur honestly, drop it; do not force it.
- v1's silver cigarette case is dropped. The painter's nervousness is
  to be carried by his hands and his sentences, not by an object.
- Keep the **opium-tainted cigarette** for Lord Henry. It is shorthand
  for him.
