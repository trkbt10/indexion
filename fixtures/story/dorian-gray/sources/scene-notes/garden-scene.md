# Scene Note — The Garden (Chapter II, middle)

A short scene, an outdoor scene, and the only one in this opening
movement that takes place outside the studio. Basil has stopped
painting; he is mixing colours indoors. Lord Henry has taken Dorian
out into the garden under the pretext of a cooler half-hour.

Function: in v1 of the outline this was the seduction scene, where
Lord Henry's speech about beauty fell on the boy under the laburnum
and broke something in him. In v2 the seduction has been moved
forward, into the studio sitting itself, and the garden is now the
scene where the boy *registers what has happened to him*. The
distinction matters in writing it. He is no longer being persuaded.
He is realising he has been persuaded.

Sensory anchor: the laburnum overhead, dropping yellow blossom on
the bench. A bee. The two green-and-white butterflies along the
path.

Dialogue: Lord Henry continues the speech he began in the studio,
but more quietly, more privately. The line about Time being jealous
of beauty has already been used inside; what is said here is its
echo. Dorian for the first time speaks back. He says only a few
sentences. He should sound, on the page, slightly drunk.

The scene ends when Basil calls from the studio that he is ready to
finish.

Working principle: do not let the garden be merely pretty. The
garden is the place where the wish is *prepared* without yet being
spoken. The flowers are accomplices.
